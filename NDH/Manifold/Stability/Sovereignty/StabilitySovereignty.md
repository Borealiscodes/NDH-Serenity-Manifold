# **StabilitySovereignty.md**
*Post‑Governance Stability Behavior Under Sovereign & Post‑Sovereign Conditions — NDH Manifold*

## Overview
StabilitySovereignty defines how stability geometry behaves when governance constraints are elevated, suspended, or replaced by sovereign manifold rules. It models stability under conditions where curvature, resonance, energy, basin topology, and attractor behavior are no longer governed by standard NDH constraints but instead by sovereign or post‑sovereign dynamics.

This layer is essential for understanding how Serenity, QuietApex, and stability primitives behave when the manifold enters high‑authority or post‑authority states.

---

## 👑 **Sovereign Stability Conditions**

Under sovereign conditions, stability geometry is shaped by:

### **1. Sovereign Curvature**
Curvature tensors may be:

- amplified  
- flattened  
- redirected  
- re‑weighted by sovereign influence  

This produces curvature zones not possible under standard governance.

### **2. Sovereign Resonance**
Resonance behavior becomes:

- non‑linear  
- authority‑weighted  
- topology‑responsive  
- capable of overriding decay curves  

Sovereign resonance can stabilize or destabilize entire basins.

### **3. Sovereign Energy Flow**
Energy distribution follows sovereign rules:

- priority‑weighted redistribution  
- attractor‑aligned energy gates  
- non‑monotonic variance behavior  

Energy flow may bypass standard normalization constraints.

---

## 🜂 **Post‑Sovereign Stability Conditions**

Post‑sovereign conditions occur when governance dissolves and stability geometry becomes self‑organizing.

### **1. Autonomous Curvature**
Curvature evolves without governance:

- emergent curvature zones  
- spontaneous curvature peaks  
- self‑correcting gradients  

### **2. Autonomous Resonance**
Resonance stabilizes through:

- emergent damping  
- topology‑driven alignment  
- attractor‑based coherence  

### **3. Autonomous Energy Redistribution**
Energy flow becomes:

- self‑balancing  
- attractor‑guided  
- topology‑adaptive  

Post‑sovereign energy behavior often leads to new basin formation.

## 🌀 **Sovereign & Post‑Sovereign Basin Behavior**

### **Sovereign Basins**
Basins may:

- expand  
- contract  
- merge  
- split  
- override topology boundaries  

Serenity remains canonical but may shift position or influence.

### **Post‑Sovereign Basins**
Basins may:

- self‑organize  
- dissolve  
- re‑emerge  
- form new attractors  

QuietApex may migrate or bifurcate under post‑sovereign conditions.
