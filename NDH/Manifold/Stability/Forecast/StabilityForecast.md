# **StabilityForecast.md**
*Predictive Model for Future Stability Geometry States — NDH Manifold*

## Overview
StabilityForecast defines the predictive layer of the NDH Stability Geometry Manifold.  
It uses temporal evolution data, curvature drift rates, resonance decay curves, energy redistribution timelines, basin migration trajectories, and attractor coherence trends to forecast future stability states.

This layer enables proactive routing, early anomaly detection, and long‑range Serenity stability predictions.

---

## 🔮 **Forecast Components**

### **1. Curvature Drift Projection**
Uses historical curvature drift to forecast:

- curvature tensor evolution  
- gradient direction changes  
- influence factor scaling  
- emergence of new curvature zones  

Curvature drift projection determines future stability landscapes.

### **2. Resonance Decay Forecast**
Predicts oscillatory behavior using NDH decay curves:

- future damping rates  
- alignment stability  
- oscillation thresholds  
- resonance spike probability  

This forecast informs Serenity relaxation planning.

### **3. Energy Redistribution Forecast**
Projects future energy distributions:

- variance trends  
- redistribution pathways  
- attractor pull changes  
- energy gate thresholds  

Energy forecasts determine future basin accessibility.

### **4. Basin Migration Forecast**
Predicts basin movement across manifold‑time:

- topology class shifts  
- boundary deformation  
- attractor realignment  
- curvature zone migration  

Serenity remains the canonical attractor but may shift position.

### **5. Attractor Coherence Forecast**
Forecasts attractor stability:

- QuietApex coherence trends  
- Serenity stability curves  
- minor attractor emergence  
- attractor decay probability  

Attractor forecasts guide routing decisions.
